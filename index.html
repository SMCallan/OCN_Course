Practical Computing, IoT & Cyber Pathway

A hands-on route where learners build things, connect things, understand digital systems and learn safer technology habits — with the starting level matched to each learner.

Core idea: learners do not automatically start at Level 2. They begin with practical starter/screening sessions using micro:bit and simple digital tasks. Most learners start at Entry 3 or Level 1, then progress naturally. Level 2 is available for learners already working at that standard, or for learners who build up to it.

Initial screening: choose the right starting point

The starter sessions are not just warm-up; they help decide the right accreditation level before registration.

Existing work

Look at previous school/college work, OCNs, Functional Skills, digital tasks or practical project evidence.

Practical confidence

Can the learner follow steps, stay with a task, troubleshoot safely and recover from small mistakes?

Evidence tolerance

Can they write short explanations, or do they need evidence captured through photos, demos and oral Q&A?

Independence

Do they need 1:1 prompting, or can they work semi-independently within a small workshop group?

Tech confidence

Can they use files, browser, device settings, simple coding tools and basic safe login habits?

Engagement and safety

Can they manage the room, equipment, peers and session boundaries safely enough for the selected level?

Staff view: delivery, mentoring and resources

For management planning: level selection, rooming, timetable, equipment, evidence and OCN registration.

Activity-led mentoring rationale

Some learners are ambivalent about formal 1:1 mentoring. A practical computing workshop creates rapport through side-by-side problem solving, shared tasks, visible progress and low-pressure conversation. The computing is the engagement vehicle; mentoring and safeguarding support happen through the activity.

Delivery model

  • Cohort: 3–6 possible; 4 likely/ideal.
  • One fixed weekday and one consistent room.
  • One lead tutor/mentor; above 6 learners needs another adult or more kit.
  • Four non-paired learner stations is the clean setup target.

SEND/accessibility design

  • Designed for learners with ASD, ADHD, ODD, SEMH needs or disrupted mainstream education histories.
  • Predictable routine, choice-based tasks, visual instructions and short wins.
  • Oral evidence and practical demonstrations where suitable.
  • High-contrast materials and tiered difficulty.

Level 2 positioning

Level 2 is not the automatic starting point. It is available for learners already working at GCSE-level applied standard, or for learners who progress there after Entry 3 / Level 1 foundation work.

How level selection works

Learners should not be placed automatically onto Level 2. Starter/screening sessions are used to assess confidence, engagement, existing work, literacy, independence, evidence tolerance and support needs. Most learners would start at Entry 3 or Level 1, then progress naturally. Learners already working at Level 2 standard can start at Level 2 if appropriate.

Portfolio evidence model

Use one digital folder per learner. Evidence should be captured during practical work: photos, screenshots, short video/demo, observation records, verbal Q&A notes, diagrams, flowcharts and brief reflections. Avoid making the course feel like an exam.
