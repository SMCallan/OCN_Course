Practical Computing, IoT & Cyber Pathway

A hands-on route where learners build things, connect things, understand how digital systems work, and learn how to use technology safely.

Core idea: start with accessible micro:bit sessions, then move into existing OCN London Level 2 units. The main accredited route is the Level 2 Award, with a clear route into the Certificate and optional coding, cyber, networking, web, game or AI extensions.

The learner journey

The micro:bit stage is the entry ramp. Formal accreditation begins when the learner is ready for the Award units.

Starter sessions

micro:bit entry ramp
Low-pressure, hands-on confidence building.

  • Learn loops and inputs visually
  • Informal, or used as evidence later where appropriate

Staff view: delivery, mentoring and resources

For Dom/management planning: approval, rooming, timetable, equipment, evidence and OCN registration.

Activity-led mentoring rationale

Some learners are ambivalent about formal 1:1 mentoring. A practical computing workshop creates rapport through side-by-side problem solving, shared tasks, visible progress and low-pressure conversation. The computing is the engagement vehicle; mentoring and safeguarding support happen through the activity.

Delivery model

  • Cohort: 3-6 learners, with 4 ideal.
  • One fixed weekday and one consistent room.
  • One lead tutor/mentor; above 6 learners needs another adult or more kit.
  • Available Monday to Friday once day/room are agreed.

SEND/accessibility design

  • Designed for learners with ASD, ADHD, ODD, SEMH needs or disrupted mainstream education histories.
  • Predictable routine, choice-based tasks, visual instructions and short wins.
  • Oral evidence and practical demonstrations where suitable.
  • High-contrast materials and tiered difficulty.

AI relationship

A separate AI awareness OCN proposal may be developed by another staff member. This practical pathway can run alongside it. If that AI route does not proceed, selected AI tools units can be added later as an optional layer.

Equipment and budget

Budget principle: the order should cover what is genuinely needed to run the pathway properly. Donated monitors/desktops/laptops can reduce spend, but the course should only rely on donated equipment once it has been checked, wiped, tested and stored.

Portfolio evidence model

Use one digital folder per learner. Evidence should be captured during practical work: photos, screenshots, short video/demo, observation records, verbal Q&A notes, diagrams, flowcharts and brief reflections. Avoid making the course feel like an exam.
